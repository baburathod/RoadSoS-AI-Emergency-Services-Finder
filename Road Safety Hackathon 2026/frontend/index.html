⚖️ Good Samaritan Law — India
The Supreme Court of India and the Ministry of Road Transport & Highways guarantee that no bystander can be detained, harassed, or held liable for taking an accident victim to hospital. You are protected — help without fear.
"A Good Samaritan shall not be liable for any civil or criminal action for any injury to or death of an accident victim, caused due to their negligence in providing emergency medical assistance." — MoRTH Guidelines, 2016
🏥 Golden Hour
The first 60 minutes after a road accident are critical. Immediate medical attention during this window dramatically increases survival rates. Call 108 or 112 immediately — every second counts.
📋 What to Tell Emergency Services
1. Your exact location (landmark / GPS / road name)
2. Number of people injured
3. Type of injuries (conscious? bleeding? breathing?)
4. Type of vehicles involved
5. Your phone number
🚦 Road Accident Reporting — Motor Vehicles Act
Under Section 134 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, the driver involved in an accident must report to the nearest police station and render assistance to the injured. Failure to do so is a punishable offence.
